## 1. EXECUTIVE BRIEF

This is not a motel website. This is a **brand homepage for Lancaster's town symbol** — a site that turns a 27-room motor lodge into the definitive place to stay in the Great North Woods. The old Lancaster Motel site was personality-rich but visually dated (BnBwebsites template, broken images, WordPress-era formatting). It had incredible content — a genuinely funny, locally-rooted voice across 10+ pages. We are keeping every ounce of that local knowledge and repackaging it in a $50k visual wrapper.

Three principles:

1. **The fox is not a logo — it is a 1913 Anna Hyatt Huntington bronze sculpture, three blocks from the front door.** Every page carries this story.
2. **Lancaster earns its place on this site.** County seat of Coös, Weeks Act birthplace, Israel River, church steeples, covered bridges, Ride the Wilds ATV hub, Santa's Village gateway. The site makes guests want to explore the town, which makes them want to stay with us.
3. **Drive-up-to-your-door is a feature, not a liability.** Park your car, bike, ATV, or snowmobile right at your room. This is the single biggest positioning advantage over every competitor — own it.

## 2. THE RED FOX INN BRAND NARRATIVE

### The Name

**Red Fox** = Lancaster's official town symbol. In 1913, for the town's 150th anniversary, Lancaster commissioned **Anna Vaughn Hyatt Huntington** — the sculptor whose *Joan of Arc* stands in Manhattan's Riverside Park — to cast a fox in bronze. It still sits in Centennial Park at Main and School, three blocks from our front door.

The town chose the fox for what it represents: **"clever, caring, independent, and at home with the wild."** That line IS our brand voice.

### The Three Pillars (our brand framework)

| Pillar | What it represents | Where it shows up |
|---|---|---|
| **The Fox** | Identity, cleverness, warmth, the town symbol | Logo, About/Story page, room names, visual identity |
| **The Forest** | The Weeks Act, White Mountain National Forest, outdoor adventure | Experience pages, seasonal content, hero photography |
| **The Town** | Lancaster's history, walkability, local food/drink scene, community | Area guide, Explore page, local recommendations |

### Brand Voice

- **Warm, knowing, slightly playful.** Like a local who knows all the best spots and can't wait to share them. The old Lancaster Motel site had this voice naturally — "Drop your trailer and your underwear," "We've stumbled across some great finds at the local antique shops." Keep that energy, polish the delivery.
- **Locally fluent.** We name Polish Princess, Granite Grind, Copper Pig, Scorpio's, the Snow Drifters. We know our town.
- **Confident but honest.** "We're not a luxury resort and we don't pretend to be. We're a warm, clean, well-run inn in one of the most beautiful small towns in America."
- **Never apologetic.** The 1955 brick building, the smaller bathrooms, the exterior corridor — these are features with character, not flaws to explain away.

- **Secondary (footer signature):** *"Clever, caring, independent, and at home with the wild."*
- **Alt options:** *"A new chapter on an old road."* · *"The fox knows Lancaster."* · *"Park at your door. Walk to everything."*

### 4.2 STORY (`/story`)

This is the most important brand page. It should feel like a magazine feature, not a corporate "About Us." Full-width sections with alternating cream/white backgrounds. Pull quotes in large italic Cormorant Garamond. Generous whitespace.

**Hero:**
- Headline: *"A New Chapter on an Old Road"*
- Subhead: *"Red Fox Inn sits at 112 Main Street in Lancaster, New Hampshire — the county seat of Coös County, the gateway to the Great North Woods, and a place where every view out the window exists because one local boy thought big."*

**Section 1: The Fox**

> "The red fox isn't just our name — it's Lancaster's official town symbol. In 1913, when Lancaster celebrated its sesquicentennial, the town commissioned sculptor Anna Vaughn Hyatt Huntington to cast a fox in bronze. Huntington was no local artisan — her *Joan of Arc* stands in Riverside Park in Manhattan, her *El Cid* guards the entrance to the Hispanic Society of America. The bronze fox she created for Lancaster still sits in Centennial Park, three blocks from our front door."

> "The town chose the fox for what it represents: clever, caring, independent, and at home with the wild. We chose the name Red Fox Inn because no other words capture this place better."

**Pull quote (large, italic, centered):**
> *"Clever, caring, independent, and at home with the wild."*

**Section 2: The Forest**

> "Every mountain you see from Lancaster exists as protected land because of a boy who grew up on one of them. John Wingate Weeks was born and raised in Lancaster, went on to become a U.S. Senator and Secretary of War, and authored the Weeks Act — signed by President Taft on March 1, 1911. That single law created the White Mountain National Forest and 47 other eastern national forests, protecting over 20 million acres."

> "Weeks's summer lodge — a 1912 fieldstone mansion with a 56-foot observation tower — is now Weeks State Park, a five-minute drive from the inn. Stand at the top of that tower and you'll see the legacy of a Lancaster kid who looked at these mountains and decided they should belong to everyone."

**Section 3: The Town**

> "Lancaster was chartered on July 6, 1763. The first settlers arrived the following spring with twenty head of cattle and twelve acres of corn seed — only to watch a freak August frost destroy the entire crop. They stayed anyway. That stubbornness runs deep here."

> "The Israel River that flows through downtown was called *Siwooganock* — 'place of the burnt pine trees' — by the Abenaki. Coös County itself takes its name from the Abenaki word for 'white pines,' and remains the only place name in America officially spelled with a diaeresis."

> "Next door to the inn, St. Paul's Episcopal Church — built in 1876 and modeled after a small English country church — is described by the Library of Congress as one of the most photographed churches in New England. The Lancaster Fair has run every year since 1870. The town's motto says it all: 'the friendly town in the friendly state.'"

**Section 4: The Colonel**

> "Colonel Edward E. Cross set type at the *Coös Democrat* newspaper in Lancaster as a fifteen-year-old, then went on to found Arizona's first newspaper, command the Fifth New Hampshire — the Union regiment that suffered the highest infantry casualties of the Civil War — and was killed at Gettysburg on July 3, 1863. His grave is a short walk from the inn."

> "When General Hancock told him that morning, 'Colonel Cross, this day will bring you a star,' Cross replied: 'No, General. This is my last battle.' He was right."

**Section 5: The Inn**

> "Red Fox Inn is a 27-room motor lodge at the center of downtown Lancaster. We're operated by Hanuram, LLC, and we're part of a family of North Country properties that includes Eastgate Inn in Littleton and Saffron Inn in Auburn, Maine. We've been in the hospitality business in this region for over eight years."

> "The building dates to the 1950s, and we honor that heritage — the exterior-corridor format means you park at your door, whether you're arriving by car, motorcycle, ATV, or snowmobile. We've upgraded every room with new mattresses, laminate wood floors, flat screen TVs, fiber optic WiFi, and modern furniture, while keeping the retro character that makes a motor lodge worth staying at."

> "We're not a luxury resort and we don't pretend to be. We're a warm, clean, well-run inn in one of the most beautiful small towns in America — run by people who live here and love it."

**Closing pull quote:**
> *"Where the North Woods welcome you home."*

## 5. VISUAL DESIGN SYSTEM

### 5.1 Colors (Red Fox Inn Brand Palette — locked in)

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Fox Red | `#C0392B` | CTAs, logo, headers, brand accent |
| Deep accent | Deep Red | `#96281B` | Hover states, dark CTA accent |
| Warm accent | Fox Orange | `#D4691A` | Thin rules, dividers, subtle highlights |
| Secondary warm | Fox Amber | `#E8A070` | Cards, tags, light accent |
| Light surface | Fox Cream | `#F5EDE4` | Card backgrounds, alternate sections |
| Body text | Fox Brown | `#2C1810` | All body copy, dark text |
| Dark background | Night | `#1C1410` | Footer, dark sections, hero overlays |
| Secondary | Forest Green | `#2D5016` | Nature accents, seasonal badges |
| Secondary light | Forest Sage | `#6B8F4E` | Tags, light green accent |
| Page background | Snow White | `#FAFAF7` | Default page background |
| Muted text | Warm Gray | `#8A8278` | Captions, meta, secondary text |

Rule: Fox Red and Fox Orange are the warm heartbeat. Forest Green is a counterpoint for nature/outdoor sections. Night for footer and dark hero overlays only.

### 5.2 Typography

- **Display:** *Cormorant Garamond* (300, 400, 500) — hero headlines, section titles, pull quotes, room names
- **Body + UI:** *DM Sans* (300, 400, 500, 600) — everything else
- **Import from Google Fonts. Never use Inter, Arial, Roboto, or system fonts.**

Pairing rule: Cormorant does headlines and emotional moments. DM Sans carries the weight of every page. Never two serif faces at the same size.

### 5.3 Imagery direction

- **Warm, not cold.** Golden-hour, warm window light, tungsten indoor light. No blue-shift daylight.
- **People occasionally, never staged.** A hand on a keycard. A couple by the fire pit. No "fake family smiling on bed."
- **Detail shots.** The threshold of a door. A coffee cup on the bedside. The fox-head door number plate. These sell trust more than wide shots.
- **Local landscape mixed in.** Mt. Prospect at dawn, the Israel River, covered bridges, fall foliage, the Centennial Park fox sculpture.
- **Consistent warm color grading.** Slightly warm, low-contrast, not oversaturated. All images should feel like the same warm autumn afternoon.
- **Seasonal rotation.** Four hero images for homepage (fall, winter, summer, spring). Swap quarterly.

### 5.4 Motion & micro-interactions (the $50k details)

- **Hero:** Slow Ken Burns pan on hero image (subtle 3% zoom over 12 seconds) OR a looping 6-second ambient video
- **Scroll reveal:** Sections fade up 16px as they enter viewport with 0.6s ease-out (IntersectionObserver or GSAP ScrollTrigger)
- **Room cards:** Hover → image zooms 4%, card lifts 4px with soft shadow bloom
- **Gallery:** Masonry layout with staggered load-in animation. Lightbox with keyboard navigation.
- **Navigation:** Sticky header that compresses on scroll (logo shrinks, bg gets slight blur/opacity)
- **Mobile menu:** Full-screen overlay with Night background, Fox Orange accent, menu items stagger in
- **Buttons:** Primary CTA has subtle inner warmth on hover (Fox Red → Deep Red with 2px glow)
- **Section dividers:** Thin Fox Orange hairline rules (0.5px), not blocks or heavy borders
- **Pull quotes:** Large Cormorant Garamond italic, Fox Red color, generous top/bottom padding (80px)
- **Phone number in header:** `tel:` linked, subtle pulse animation on mobile

Rule: if a motion doesn't serve comprehension or delight, cut it. No motion for motion's sake.

### 5.5 Layout principles

- **Generous whitespace.** 96px section padding desktop, 64px mobile. This is the single biggest differentiator from the old site.
- **Max content width 1280px, text columns 640-720px.**
- **Asymmetric hero.** Not always centered — image on one side, text offset on the other.
- **Mobile-first.** Every section designed for 375px first, scaled up. More phone visitors than desktop.
- **Full-bleed imagery** alternating with contained text sections. Rhythm: full → contained → full → contained.
